Charleston's Calhoun Mansion

Centuries of Charleston Depicted in Victorian Architecture

While an up and coming coastal town full of history, Charleston, South Carolina is home to numerous decadent mansions filled with vibrant colors and enamouring architecture. One grande home known for its stakeholding place in the area is the Calhoun Mansion.

image source credit: https://www.flickr.com/photos/johnwerry/6999084674/

Specs

The house itself features several spectacular amenities and detail not always caught by the eye. Here are some quick facts to brush up on.

  • The historic mansion was built in 1876 with 24,000 square feet with a total of thirty main rooms and a ballroom whose ceiling reaches a grand total of 45 feet tall.
  • Built initially for the highly regarded George W. Williams, a businessman in the Charleston area, it was passed through the family and later turned into a hotel in the 20th century.
  • The building cost of this magnificent mansion back in the days of 1876 totaled to a mere sum nowadays of $240,000.
  • The detail within the house consists of delicate and ornate plaster, wood mouldings, premiere chandeliers, and has 23 period fireplaces.
  • The cherry on top of this unique and timeless mansion includes the beautifully intricate decor designed and installed by Louis Comfort Tiffany.
